Requirement Analysis

During the project week for the Requirement Analysis course, our team of six people did the analysis of the requirements our client provided. We started by reviewing the reports of the other students who had the same assignment during the semester. Then we had a short meeting with the client to ask questions. After that it was time to make the use case diagram (first picture) and a data model (second picture) with the team. Then we started the individual work, namely we started working on the prototypes. This is what the application we are going to create should look like. I have attached the pictures of some of the prototypes I made. We had a team meeting to discuss the look and functions of the prototypes to make them look similar, and we made a few agreements and changes. Once that was finished, we could start working on the document, where we listed all the functionalities from the use case diagram with the data model view and the prototype.

I learned how to talk with a client in a professional way, but also how to translate the customer's requirements into actual functionalities and prototypes.

PHP

We had a course called PHP in the first semester of the second year, in the second semester we have a big project using PHP. This is a group project and our team consists out of five people. This projects builds further on the Requirement Analysis project since it is the same subject: the staff management system for a family in England. They wanted an application where they can do many things, such as keeping track of absences, planning meals where the staff can subscribe to, managing tasks and appointments, uploading invoices and files... I provided the images of the pages I made and I also did some small styling changes. In the meal plans page they can see the scheduled meals and add, edit or delete meal plans. The "submit invoice" page is of course for submitting invoices and the edit profile page is to edit your own profile. Lastly, the "declare absence" page is for declaring when you will be absent. This project isn't finished yet.

I learned how to talk with a client in a professional way, to listen to what they want and how to work in a group. Of course, I also learned more about Laravel 9, which is used in this project.
